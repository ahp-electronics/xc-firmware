Changing PLL Configuration

PLL (Phase-locked loops) are used to modify and generate clock signals to meet design requirements. Additionally, PLLs are used for distributing clock signals to different devices in a design, reducing clock skew between devices, improving I/O timing, and generating internal clock signals.

When you open the Logic Block view for a PLL element, a tabular view opens, which enables you to inspect how a PLL is configured within your design. For example, you can view which control signals of the PLL are used along with the settings for your PLL.

The PLL utilizes several attributes that allow the configuration of the PLL through source constraints and preference files. You can change those attributes with the Logic Block view.

See the FPGA Libraries Manual for details on attribute settings of individual PLL design elements. You can also refer to the following Technical Notes on details of PLL attributes and their usage.

LatticeECP2/M sysCLOCK PLL/DLL Design and Usage Guide (TN1103)
LatticeECP3 sysCLOCK PLL/DLL Design and Usage Guide (TN1178)
LatticeSC sysCLOCK and PLL/DLL User’s Guide (TN1098)
LatticeECP/EC and LatticeXP sysCLOCK PLL Design and Usage Guide (TN1049)
MachXO sysCLOCK PLL Design and Usage Guide (TN1089)
MachXO2 sysCLOCK PLL Design and Usage Guide (TN1199)

You should do a DRC check after making changes. Click the DRC button on the Logic Block view toolbar. DRC results appear in the Tcl console.
