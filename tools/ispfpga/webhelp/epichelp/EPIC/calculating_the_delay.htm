Calculating the Delay

Delay is the time that a signal takes to reach a load pin from a driver pin. You can calculate the delay associated with load pins and driver pins in a given net or path in EPIC. Delay information is displayed in the Tcl console. When otherwise not indicated, delay values are given in nanoseconds.

To calculate delay on paths or nets:
1. Select a net or two specific pins of a path or a net.
2. Choose Design > Delay, or click the delay button on the Actions panel.

In path delay calculation, if there are multiple paths between the two pins, the path with the maximum delay is shown.

You can also click the attribute button in the Actions panel to view the pin list and associated delays in the Net Attributes Dialog Box. If there is a blank next to a pin name (either in the Pin List or in the Tcl console), it means either that the pin is the net driver (which has no delay) or that the pin is unrouted.

If the net is fully or partially routed, a list of pins appears in the Tcl console, along with their associated delays. If the net is fully unrouted, each pin is listed as “unrouted.”
