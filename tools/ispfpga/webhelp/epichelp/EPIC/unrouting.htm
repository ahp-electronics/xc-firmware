Unrouting

Unrouting rips up the routed connections for specified objects. The logical connections remain after unrouting. If the Ratsnests layer is enabled on the Layers toolbar, the ripped-up routes will be displayed as ratsnest lines. You can unroute selected objects, or you can unroute the entire design. Note that locked nets cannot be unrouted.

You can unroute the following objects:

Components
Nets
Net Pins
Route Segments
Macros
Used local lines, long lines and pin wires
To unroute selected objects:
1. Select the objects to unroute.
You can select any combination of components, nets, net pins, route segments, macros, and used wires. Components, net pins, route segments, macros, and used wires can be selected in the editing area of the main view. Nets and macros can be selected in the Object menu.
2. Choose Design > Route > Unroute or click the unroute button on the Actions panel.
The objects you selected are unrouted. If the Ratsnests layer is enabled, the previously routed connections will be displayed as ratsnest lines.
To unroute the entire design:
Choose Design > Route > Unroute All.
All routed connections in the design are unrouted except the locked signals and certain macro nets.

When you unroute a net pin, only the connection to the net pin is unrouted. All other routed connections on the net remain. When you unroute a net, you unroute the entire net. When you unroute a component, you unroute each net pin on the component.
