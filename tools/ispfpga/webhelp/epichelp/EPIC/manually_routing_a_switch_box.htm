Manually Routing a Switch Box

Switch boxes are located at the junctions of horizontal and vertical local lines and can be used to connect local lines. The switch boxes have certain allowable matrices for pin connections. Allowable routes vary, depending on the location of the switch box on the chip. For example, switch boxes situated on the perimeter of the chip (top, bottom, right and left as viewed from EPIC) will have fewer pins; therefore the routing matrices will be different from those on switch boxes located inside the perimeter.

To see the permissible paths for any pin in a given switch box, click the mouse once on the pin. The switch box looks similar to the graphic below. To deselect the pin and clear the paths from the screen, click once again on the pin or select View > Unhighlight All. Only one switch box path can be displayed at one time within a single switch box. All other paths are cleared when a new one is selected.

To route a connection through a switch box:
Select the local wires leading to the pins you want to connect and click route on the Actions panel.

A “bank shot” is a way of indirectly connecting two switch box pins that cannot be connected directly. If you want to route a bank shot through a switch box, you must select (in the correct order) the local lines leading to all of the pins you want to connect.

To route a bank shot through a switchbox:
Select the local lines leading to the pins in the order you want to connect and perform the Route command.
